DENVER CRIME DATA

Diving down into crimes happening in the City and County of Denver, how it is divided by neighborhoods, time of the year and number of the precincts in that area.

The Broad View

How different crimes stack up in 2019

Looking at the broad picture of how diffrent crimes stack up over the year by plotting an area graph using sequential color ordering for number of crimes, traffic accident was the most reported crime type over the year with minor monthly variations. This is followed by larceny, public-disorder and auto theft as the top crimes. There is a consistent trend of number of crimes dipping down during winters to spring with a major dip somewhere in January. Whereas the number of crimes significantly increases when we move to warmer months which is expected. Another interesting finding is that the number of reported traffic accidents increased significantly in the winter months, starting from mid-September all the way to mid-January which is understable attributing to snowy weather.

Crime Across Neighborhoods

Diving in: Most notorious neighborhoods

If we plot the reported incidents of crimes using the geographical coordinates and use sequential color encoding along with size to show number of crimes based on different neighborhoods, we end up with a map like this. As the color goes from lighter to darker and the size of the circle becomes bigger, it corresponds to increasing number of reported crimes. A quick glance at the viz show that five-points is the most notorious neighborhood of the city, followed by stapleton and capitol hill. This can be attributed to a large number of factors like population, poverty and homelessness. A deeper dive into the neighborhood of five-points and seeing factors like most popular crimes, number of precincts, division of crimes in a precinct will give us a better idea of the crime landscape of the city.

Crime Landscape

An overview of crime landscape of the city

If we take a deeper look at how crimes are distributed throughout the city by color coding the different types of crime and using circle size an indicator of number of crimes of a specific category, we end with a map like this. Another detail worth noticing in this visualization is that the tool tip shows us the number of precincts in a particular area which contextually informs a lot about factors that affect the crime landscape, as we will discuss in the later visualizations. By observing the visualization, we find that, when zoomed into five points, the most notorious neighborhood according to our previous visualization, traffic accident is the most reported crime, followed by drug alcohol. Types of Crime

How diffrent crimes stack up in five points

If we plot the number of diffrent crimes happening at five points, and plot them using number of crimes per category as the size of the circle. We arrive at the conclusion that the top five crimes happening at five points are traffic accident, drug-alcohol, public-disorder, larceny and motor-vehicle theft. As observed by the previous visualization, five points have 4 precincts. Traffic Accidents Across Precincts

How traffic accidents are divided between precincts over the year

Diving even deeper into studying the crime landscape of five points, we plot the most reported crime i.e. traffic accident in the neighborhood, divided among the four precincts that make the neighborhood, throughout the year. This gives us several interesting insights, like September has the most number of reported traffic accidents and the precincts 612, 621 and 211 contribute towards majority of reports. But get a better idea of how different crimes are reported at different precincts we might have to take a step back and look at the bigger picture including all crimes and not just traffic accidents.

Crimes Across Precincts

Distribution of crimes across precincts over the year

Although traffic accidents, the most reported crime in the neighborhood of five points is a good measure to study diffrent precincts, a bigger picture, combining all the crimes that happen in five points gives a clear understanding of how precincts handle the crimes. The plot of all the crimes over the year confirms certain previously observed trends, such as peaking of crimes in warmer months and dipping down of crimes in colder months. This look at the bigger picture gives us some pretty interesting new insights, which will be really instrumental in understanding how precincts handle crimes. Precincts 211, 621 and 612 have the most number of reported crimes in the neighborhood of five points. This can be attributed to a variety of factors such as population density, shortage of officials, poverty etc. An imortant way how this insight can be used is that the authorities responsible can mend the disparity between reported crimes in the neighborhood by focusing more on that specific precinct and the effects of the solution can trickle down, improving the entire crime landscape of the city.

Precinct 211

How crime varies in the most notorious precinct of the city

We further take a closer look of how crime varies in the most notorious precinct of the city, precinct 211. For this visualization, we take the five most reported crimes from our Types of Crime visualization namely traffic accident, drug-alcohol, public-disorder, larceny and motor-vehicle theft. We then plot these against the reported date to get an idea how they vary across the year. Few interesting insights to deduce are, exactly as expected both traffic accident and motor-vehicle theft peak in mid-August and usually stay high in the warmer months and dip in the colder months. Another really important insight is that the public disorder and larceny also have comparable number of reports as traffic-accident and that's something the concerned city authorities can work on curbing at the root level. Drug-alcohol related reports see an up-down throughout the year and that's something worth nothing and needs attention throughout the year.
